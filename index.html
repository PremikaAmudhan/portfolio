About Me

I’m a public health researcher and data storyteller who works at the intersection of epidemiology, environmental resilience, and community-centered design. My work spans GIS analysis, statistical modeling, climate adaptation metrics, and visual communication—translating complex data into tools and insights that help people make smarter, healthier decisions.

I chose public health because I’ve always been drawn to work that improves people’s lives at scale.
The ability to merge science, data, and community-centered thinking felt deeply meaningful to me. As I learned more about health inequities, environmental risks, and systemic barriers, I realized how much impact thoughtful public health work can create.
This field gives me a path to use analytical and creative skills, together translating complex problems into solutions that genuinely help people. That combination of purpose and possibility is what motivates my journey.

My experience spans research, data visualization, GIS analysis, and policy communication across environmental health, infectious disease, and community-based projects.
I’ve worked on climate adaptation metrics for NYC, stunting analyses, mental-health resources for students, and environmental risk assessments. These projects helped me apply statistical tools, narrative design, and field insights in real-world contexts.
I’ve also learned how to synthesize scientific evidence into clear, decision-ready outputs for diverse audiences. Every project strengthened my understanding of how public health systems can be designed to work better for the people they serve.

I envision a future where public health is proactive, data-informed, and deeply rooted in equity. I want to help build systems that anticipate risks whether environmental, social, or structural and empower communities before harm occurs.
My goal is to blend analytics, communication, and policy strategy to design interventions that are both evidence-based and accessible.
I hope to contribute to a world where people not only survive but thrive, supported by environments and infrastructures that enable healthier choices and better outcomes for all.
